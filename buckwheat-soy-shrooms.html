buckwheat with mushrooms, egg and... something unexpected

by pash bespaloff

Buckwheat is unrealistically healthy. In recent years, the word "superfood" has been used to refer to such expensive cereals as quinoa and amaranth, while buckwheat is somehow forgotten. But it's forgetten for nothing. See for yourself:

  • Slow carbs (you'll be full for a long time)
  • Low calories (110 kcal per 100 grams of boiled buckwheat)
  • Lots of vitamins (group B, E, PP, as well as potassium, calcium, phosphorus, iron, zinc, and iodine)
  • Essential amino acids and fiber
  • Plenty of easily digestible vegetable protein (almost twice as much as in rice)
  • Buckwheat is not a cereal, but a flower grain, so it does not contain gluten
  • In its finished form buckwheat almost does not lose its useful properties (unlike, for example, rice)
  • Low price

Already want to boil a bucket of buckwheat? But in its pure form it will bore you pretty quickly. And that's where an unexpected ingredient comes in — soy sauce!

I do not know how it happened, but the buckwheat and soy sauce, like the Russian and Chinese, are brothers forever. I'm sharing a simple and healthy recipe that I'm finishing up right now.

for 1 serving you will need:

  • Buckwheat — 50 g
  • Water — 120 ml
  • Mushrooms — 100 g
  • Yellow onion — 1 small or 1/2 of a medium
  • Carrot — 1/2 of a medium (it should be about the size of an onion, but a little smaller)
  • Soy sauce or Maggi sauce — 1–3 tablespoons (to taste)
  • Vegetable oil — 2 tablespoons
  • Butter — 50 g
  • Hard-boiled egg — 1
  • Black pepper — to taste
  • Green onion — 1 nib (to taste)
  • Sour cream (15–20% of fat) — 1 teaspoon (to taste)

the cooking

  1. Prepare the ingredients. Cut onion into quarter rings, carrots into small dice (pea-size), mushrooms in half and then into thin slices of 4-5 mm. Boil the egg ≈6 minutes from water start boiling. When the egg is ready, transfer it to cold water for 5-10 minutes — then it will be easier to peel.

  2. On medium-high heat (7/10 on electric stove) in a pot or deep frying pan, melt the butter, add 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil, so that the butter does not burn, and throw the mushrooms. Fry for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

    When the mushrooms begin to release liquid, reduce the heat to 5/10 and continue to cook them, stirring every 2-3 minutes. They will gradually darken and decrease in size, which is what we need — their flavor will enhance. When the mushrooms take on a deep dark brown color — they are ready, put them away for now.

  3. Add vegetable oil to the frying pan, return the heat to 7/10. When the oil is hot - send the onions to fry. It is necessary to fry slow, 15-20 minutes, for onions to become caramel brown. Stir it every 2-3 minutes, and if necessary, reduce the heat by 1-2 points. 10 minutes after adding the onions to the pan carrots are about to go, it should be fried with the onions for 5-10 minutes, to your liking.

  4. Rinse buckwheat 4-5 times under running water, add it to the onion and carrots, fry a little until you smell it. Then add the mushrooms back, pour soy sauce and water, bring to boil, cover with a lid, lower the heat to low simmer and cook for 15 minutes. Then turn off the heat, add some more butter and leave under a lid for 10 minutes.

  5. Mix buckwheat with chopped egg, add freshly ground black pepper and small chopped green onion to taste. No salt, the soy sauce is salty enough as it is. Right before serving add a heaping teaspoon of sour cream.

You can prepare a lot at once and store in the refrigerator — just measure 2 parts water to 1 part dry buckwheat before cooking. The amount of other ingredients increase proportionally. If you are going to cook for several times — don't add the egg at once, boil a few in advance and store them in the refrigerator. The same for green onions and sour cream.

Before serving, it's a good thought to "refresh" the buckwheat with an additional couple of drops of soy sauce.

By the way, if you are gluten-free and therefore often eat buckwheat, this recipe will also work for you, there is soy sauce without gluten nowadays.
